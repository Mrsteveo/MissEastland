WHO WE ARE

OUR GOALS

ADVOCACY, EDUCATION AND RECOGNITION FOR YOUTH

The charitable organization of Miss Eastlands provides opportunities for advocacy, education and recognition for youth, young women and girls who want to positively impact our community. Miss Eastlands is an organization advocating the “Cultivate One Love Movement” within the Nairobi’s Eastlands.
Our aim is to empower disadvantaged young people to break cycles of poverty and lead change in their families and communities. We envision a society where young people enjoy their rights and contribute to equitable and sustainable development in Eastlands. We do this by nurturing a nature and peace-loving soul, above language and tribal differences, through the loving and the goodness of young men and women.
We raise a call to the society on a large scale, through the annual Mr & Miss Eastlands Beauty Pageant and the Bodybuilding Award and through the Miss Eastlands Organization we have in the past partnered with the office of the president, Embakasi district, County government of Nairobi and World Vision Organization on causes such as national cohesion and peace building while also enabling program Ambassadors to conduct individualized projects in their communities.

VISION

To have self-reliant and resilient youth who contribute to equitable and sustainable development.

EMPOWERMENT, PHILANTROPHY AND SELF CONFIDENCE
__________

RUN BY YOUTH FOR YOUTH

MEO empowers youth to realize their ambition and build self-confidence, acting as a catalyst for future success. The MEO community is a brotherhood and sisterhood that is committed to uplifting and empowering one another

CREATING OPPORTUNITIES

The delegates and titleholders that have participated in the MEO system are able to cultivate their personal, professional, and philanthropic goals. These young men and women are forward thinking and motivated to not just talk about change, but to initiate it.

BREAKING STEREOTYPES

Research has shown the #1 obstacle for young men and women to overcome in reaching their potential is a lack of self- confidence. By developing self- confidence through MEO experiences, young men and women have gone onto high- profile careers in business, government, finance, broadcasting and entertainment.
